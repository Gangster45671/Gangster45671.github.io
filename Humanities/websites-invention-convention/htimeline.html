Timeline of Internet Hardware Development

  • 1962

    Internet Conceptualized

    Leonard Kleinrock published a paper called "Information Flow in Large Communication Nets". Then Elemer Shapiro, Steve Carr, Stever Crocker, Jeff Rulifson, Ron Stoughton, Paul Baran, Thomas Marill, Lawrence Roberts, Barry Wessler and others developed the specifications for internet communication. And helped to developed the ARPANET.

  • 1969

    ARPANET Built

    The internet began to grow. It now consited of 4 universities in the United States.

  • 1974

    Commercial Internet

    The ARPANET was now made commercial, and renamed to Telnet. This was because Bob Metcalfe developed ethernet, which allowed industries to connect to this network.

  • 1984

    The modem, DNS, TCP/IP

    Dennis Hayes and Dale Herrington made the modem on 1977, this allowed home users to connect to the internet but only if they had an ethernet connection. Then Danny Cohen, David Reed and John Shoch revised the internet protocal to TCP/IP which is what is still used today. At this point DNS(Domain Name System) was also created by Paul Mockapetris and Jon Postel. This allowed website to have human-readable adresses(for example 'google.com') instead of the more oldschool ip address(216.58.199.14)

  • 1993

    Dial Up, the first Web Browser

    This is when the internet became more simmiliar to the internet we know today. By 1993 a company called "The World" was providing a Dial-Up internet service. Users at home would connect their modems to the telephone line, and then be able to access the internet. HTML(Hyper Text Markup Language) was made by Tim Berners-Lee, which allowed for element-based graphical pages to be displayed. He also made the first website, it's still works today. Visit. At this time the first web browser known as 'Mosaic' also came about.
